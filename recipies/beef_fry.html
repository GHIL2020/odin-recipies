Sichuan Beef Stir fry

Szechuan beef has a sweet and spicy flavor, and Mongolian beef has a mild taste. Szechuan beef hails from the Sichuan Province in southwestern China. Its main ingredients are chili peppers, garlic, and Szechuan peppercorn. It has a sweet/spicy and complicated taste.

The Szechuan sauce is made of Whisked broth, tomato paste, vinegar, sugar, soy sauce, oil, cornstarch and red pepper in a small bowl.

Ingredients

Marinade

  • 1 flank steak
  • 1 egg , beaten
  • 1 tablespoon peanut oil (or vegetable oil)
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt

Sauce

  • 3 tablespoons Chaikiang vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons of Shaoxing wine
  • 1 tablespoon of light soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of dark soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of homemade chili oil
  • 1 tablespoon of Dooubanjiang
  • 1/2 cup of beef stock
  • 3 tablespoons of sugar
  • 1 tablespoon of cornstarch

Stir-fry

  • 1/3 cup of cornstarch
  • 1/3 cup of peanut oil
  • 1/4 white onion
  • 1 bell pepper
  • 4 chinese dried chili peppers
  • 1 tablespoon ginger
  • 1 tablespoon garlic
  • Roasted sesame seeds

Steps

Marinate the beef

  1. Beat the egg in a medium sized bowl. Add the sliced beef, vegetable oil, and salt. Mix well and let marinate for 15 to 20 minutes while preparing the other ingredients.

Mix the sauce

  1. Combine everything for the sauce in a big bowl and mix well.

Stir fry

  1. When youâ€™re ready to cook, drain the extra liquid from the bowl of beef. Add the cornstarch. Stir to coat the beef, until it forms an uneven coating with a little dry cornstarch left unattached.
  2. Heat oil in a large nonstick skillet (or a cast iron pan) over medium high heat until hot. Add the beef and spread into a single layer in the skillet. Separate the beef pieces with a pair of tongs or chopsticks.
  3. Cook without touching the beef for 2 to 3 minutes, or until the bottom turns golden. Flip to brown the other side, 1 to 2 misnutes. Transfer the beef to a big plate and remove the pan from the stove. Let cool for 2 to 3 minutes
  4. Place the pan back onto the stove and turn to medium heat. You should still have 1 to 2 tablespoons of oil in the pan. If not, add more.
  5. Add the onion, pepper, and dried chili pepper. Cook and stir for 1 minut
  6. Add the ginger and garlic. Stir a few times to release the fragrance.
  7. Stir the sauce again to dissolve the cornstarch completely. Pour into the pan. Stir and cook until it thickens a bit.
  8. Add back the beef pieces. Stir to coat beef with sauce.
  9. Transfer everything to a plate immediately and garnish with roasted sesame seeds, if using.
  10. Serve hot over steamed rice as a main dish.
